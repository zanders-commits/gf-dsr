Section 02 / Business Case + Proposed Solution

The Problem You're Solving — and How Reeco Closes It.

Three pain points. Three direct answers.

01
The Problem

Double-Entry Finance Work Draining Your Team

Every GF property manager maintains a manual PO log or Excel checkbook, then turns around and re-enters that same data into ProfitSword. Your finance team does the work twice — once to control spending, once to report it. That's not a process problem. It's a structural gap in your tools.

The Answer

One Entry. Two Systems. Live.

Reeco pushes open PO data directly into ProfitSword's committed-spend area in real time. The moment an order is placed in Reeco, it's visible in ProfitSword's checkbook view — no manual re-entry, no lag, no reconciliation at month-end.

02
The Problem

AP That Requires Babysitting

Current systems can automate invoices until a line item touches multiple GL codes — then the logic breaks and someone has to step in. If your AP tool can't tell the difference between Hormel going to complimentary breakfast and Berry Foods going to cost of food sales, you don't have automated AP. You have AP you have to supervise.

The Answer

Intelligent GL Coding That Learns

Reeco codes invoices by role and item — not just by vendor. The housekeeping director ordering gloves codes to a different GL than the F&B director ordering the same item. The system learns each user's coding pattern over time, so it gets faster and more accurate with every invoice processed. Unlimited invoices, no per-invoice charge.

03
The Problem

One Platform That Has to Work for Every Property

A 78-room Fairfield in a tertiary market running $109 ADR has completely different economics than a 700-key Renaissance Baltimore doing $5M a month. Most platforms are built for one or the other. Flat enterprise pricing for a portfolio this diverse doesn't pencil — and neither does a tool too complex for a limited-service front desk.

The Answer

Module Flexibility. Flat Per-Property Fee.

Reeco prices per property, not per user. Unlimited seats are included. Full-service properties can run Purchasing + AP + Inventory + Culinary; limited-service properties get Purchasing + AP at a rate that works for a tertiary market. The platform adapts to the property — not the other way around.

Section 07 / FAQs

Questions We Expect — Answered Directly.

If something's missing, email Zander and we'll add it before the proposal meeting.

"We just finished a major implementation year with M3 and ProfitSword. We don't want another botched rollout."
Understood — and we take that seriously. Reeco does not require a dedicated full-time resource to manage implementation. Because GF is already an M3 and ProfitSword customer, both integrations configure in days, not weeks. There's no re-mapping of your chart of accounts, no new GL structure — your USAli v12 standardization works with Reeco exactly as-is. We'll present a full, property-specific rollout plan at the proposal meeting.
"Will the ProfitSword integration actually replace the manual checkbook process?"
Yes — and here's exactly how it works. The moment a purchase order is placed in Reeco, the open PO data flows into ProfitSword's tentative/committed spend area. Your GMs see the checkbook view in ProfitSword complete — posted actuals, open commitments, and forecasted spend in one place — without any manual double-entry. Once invoices process, they go to both M3 and ProfitSword simultaneously. The invoice image is accessible directly inside ProfitSword when you drill into a GL code.
"How does the approval workflow handle a diverse portfolio — from a highway hotel to a flagship property?"
Approvals are configured per property, not globally. Each property can have independent thresholds — trigger on dollar amount, percentage over forecast, or both. You can also set notifications without requiring formal approval if you want visibility without slowing operations down. Out-of-office approval delegation is on the product roadmap; the current workaround is adding a backup approver at the same level. We'll include roadmap timing in the proposal materials.
"What about property-level access and regional views for our operations team?"
Every user is given access to the specific properties they're responsible for. A regional VP can be scoped to their cluster of hotels with a dynamic filter view. Corporate-level portfolio grouping (regional P&L-style views) is on the near-term roadmap — the data exists in the system today, it's a filtering and display layer. We'll present the current state and roadmap at the proposal meeting.
